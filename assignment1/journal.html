Master Mobile UX Video

Intro:

Design for the smallest and least powerful device first! Things are changing so fast that we have to program for lots of different kinds of devices. 2007 introduced a new era for mobile devices. Different screens sizes with different resolutions. We have to keep this in mind so we can provide for all of these! Replacements of these devices are also being replaced every 1-4 years. Instead of having a single device for a family, now individuals have multiple devices. Retro-fitting for mobile might save time but it can cause issues with size, bandwidth, etc. Start with mobile first. This might be time consuming and costly, but it is much more effective long term. Think of how many people you see walking around with their heads in their phones? This is the new paradigm shift. How many people do you see walking around with their laptops? This is not a fad!

Users:

People don't know what they want until you show it to them!


1. Be there:
They expect you to be there and help them through questions.

2. Be Useful:
Only 9% of users stay if they aren't satisfied with help of there needs. They have to receive what they came for as quickly as possible or they will leave. Provide them with what they came for as quickly as possible when they arrive.

3. Be Quick:
Reduce their cognitive load! Not just fast loading but serious functionality as well. Smooth and seamless interaction. If it takes 5 steps... it's too much! Make it 0 steps for them. Have it do something in background. Think of how they are holding the device. Using a one handed grip with a thumb to reach things. The larger the device, the more you have to decide on UI/UX to create functionality. Loading is a big deal too! 1/2 second delay could mean 20% drop in traffic. 2 seconds could cost 4% drop in revenue! The faster it loads the more likely they are to stay and use your app longer. Networks are crucial! Not everyone has the latest and greatest! So make sure you are creating the fastest speeds.

Speed 1. reduce image usage! small images for small devices and shorter loading times and resolution when needed.
2. SVG/Webfonts are lower in bandwidth
3. Optimize and Miniamize CSS and JS files. 4. GZIP files to eliminate unneccesary data usuage


Primary Controls:

Be empathic to users needs! Menu/Primary controls should be where it's easily accessed! Menu and Subnavigation also at the bottom of the screen for mobile users with there single and hold and reachablity with thumb. It makes the app seem faster which increases the use of the app and the user satisfaction. Hamburger menu still not known 100% as the menu button. Create a "button" or a wrapped word to draw the users to the area. Just the word menu wrapped in a border has the highest success rate.

Mobile Inputs:Forms

Make more focused simplied solutions. There is less screen, so we need to reduce that cognitive load! Make sure when you make forms that you only ask for information that is required! Put the labels inside the type field so that you can use less space. Try to avoid confusion by disapearing words. Let them appear above while in use. Make check boxes large for phone/thumb. Make what they want appear simple and quickly. Break it into quick simple sections with an obvious end point. Allow Auto complete. Allow a hide or show password button. Default show. It doesn't decrease security. Space credit card numbers to create clarity. If you are needing a number imput, make sure you use the number pad! Make phone numbers anchors to they can quickly tap and call without having to type it in.

Mobile Advertising:

Do your research! How do users use their devices! NO POP UPS!!!!!! If adding ads, make them small and simple. Make it revealable, but keep it small. Cool! you can put a window behind the article that has the add sticky to the page so you scroll up the add while you scroll through the article. Keep it simple!!!
